4. Minimum Viable Product

I've borrowed the titular term from Agile project management.

At least thats where I've heard it.
Because of the time issue I talked about you will be under time pressure to learn any coding, stats, maths and anything else you might need in a very short period of time.
If you have a particularly ambitious project. You have to realise this trade off.

And then decide what the skeleton version or the minimum viable version of your project looks like.

If you have a proof of concept that adds value you can always build on it. Bells and whistles can wait.
